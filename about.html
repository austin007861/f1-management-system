About the project

Built by a fan,
for the paddock.

Paddock Pulse started as a weekend project: a single page tracking the Australian Grand Prix result. Six months and one championship shake-up later, it's grown into a full season companion.

Our mission

Make the sport easier to follow

Formula 1 moves fast — both on track and off it. New power units, two new manufacturers, and a championship lead that has changed hands more than once already make 2026 the most unpredictable season in years. Paddock Pulse exists to keep that story straight: who's leading, who's chasing, and what's coming up next.

This site is an independent, fan-built project. It is not affiliated with Formula 1, the FIA, or any competing team — it's simply a place to check the standings, browse the grid, and read a quick recap between sessions.

What's new for 2026

A genuinely new era

Active aerodynamics & new power units

Cars now run a revised hybrid power unit with a much higher share of electrical power, paired with active aero systems that adjust drag on the straights and downforce in the corners — all on 100% sustainable fuel.

Audi arrives as a full works team

Audi has taken over the former Sauber entry to become a full works constructor, fielding Nico Hülkenberg and Gabriel Bortoleto.

Cadillac joins as the 11th team

Backed by General Motors, Cadillac becomes Formula 1's first new team since Haas in 2016, fielding Sergio Pérez and Valtteri Bottas on Ferrari power units while it develops its own engine for 2029.
