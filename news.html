Featured · Round 7 Recap

Hamilton breaks through in Barcelona — and the title fight tightens

For six rounds, the 2026 season belonged almost entirely to Mercedes. Kimi Antonelli's rookie campaign opened with a run of consecutive victories that no driver in the sport's history had managed before him, and by the time the grid reached Monaco, the championship picture looked close to settled.

Barcelona-Catalunya told a different story. Lewis Hamilton, still searching for the form that defined his Ferrari move a year earlier, converted a strong qualifying lap into his first win in red, fending off pressure on a track that has historically punished mistakes. It was enough to lift him into second in the standings, 41 points behind Antonelli with fifteen rounds still to run.

The result also mattered for Ferrari's constructors' campaign, closing the gap to Mercedes slightly and giving Charles Leclerc a stronger platform to build from heading into the Austrian double-header. With McLaren and Red Bull both still searching for their first win of the season, the 2026 title fight is far from decided.
